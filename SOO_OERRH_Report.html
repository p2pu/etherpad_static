OERRH Hypotheses explored - http://oerresearchhub.org/collaborative-research/hypotheses/
Directly
  • Informal learners use a variety of indicators when selecting OER
  • Informal means of assessment are motivators to learning with OER
  • Use of OER leads to critical reflection by educators, with evidence of improvement in their practice
Tangentially, missing comparison piece
  • Open education acts as a bridge to formal education, and is complementary, not competitive, with it
  • Informal  learners adopt a variety of techniques to compensate for the lack of  formal support, which can be supported in open courses
  • Use of OER leads to improvement in student performance and satisfaction.
  • The open aspect of OER creates different usage and adoption patterns than other online resources (question about attitudes?)

Additional research questions explored:
  • How do participants evaluate new open content, tools, and services? (question about how they dealt w/new tools in post)
  • What needs are the open content, tools, and services addressing? In what ways? (reason they're taking the course? were their needs met in post)
  • How do participants engage with each other through OER or other tools used in the course? (web data)
  • What motivates learning in the course? (questions on badging) 
  • What fears are addressed and mitigated in the process of learning with and about OER? (Q18)

Report should include:
  • Raw data - links/access

  • Research methods

  • Summary/findings (objective)
  • Gaps, missing data (eg. comparison study)
  • Lessons learned
  • Recommendations for further research, increasing participant feedback, data collection
  • New hypotheses/questions generated
  • Other conclusions
