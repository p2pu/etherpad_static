Cyberlearning: Transforming Education (NSF)

TOPIC: The National Science Foundation is inviting grant applications
for projects to integrate advances in technology with advances in what
is known about how people learn. Of particular interest are
technological advances that allow more personalized learning
experiences, draw in and promote learning among those in populations
not currently served well by current educational practices, allow
access to learning resources anytime and anywhere, and provide new
ways of assessing capabilities. Cyberlearning research should shed
light on how technology can enable new forms of educational practice,
and broad implementation of its findings should result in a more
actively-engaged and productive citizenry and workforce.
DEADLINE: For Exploration projects (EXP) and Design and Implementation
projects (DIP), full proposals are due January 17. For Integration and
Deployment projects (INDP), a letter of intent is due May 14, full
proposals July 14.
FUNDS: $30 million is available for 24 to 34 awards: 14 to 18 EXPs, 7
to 12 DIPs, and 2 to 4 INDPs.
ELIGIBILITY: An individual may participate as PI or Co-PI in no more
than three proposals: at most, two proposals in the EXP and DIP
categories combined, and at most, one proposal in the INDP category.
