  • Proposed Project 2 - Courses that start at conferences.  Replicable revenue generating model.
    • First P2PU SoSI course is still the most successful
    • Based around local teams - working in their own local context
    • Presented at a conference to create some excitement
    • Then offered an opportunity for participants to get involved
    • Formed relationship face-to-face during the conference
    • Model is:
      • Offer as service to conference organizers who want to replicate this model
      • Recruit course organizers 2 months before through a webinar
      • Work with them before the conference
      • Speak at the conference
      • Lead a workshop to help organizers get ideas and recruit participants
    • Examples:
      • Ashoka U Exchange
      • Compass Partners Conference
      • Global Business School Network Conference
      • Aspen Network of Development Entrepreneurs Conference
      • SoCap Conference
      • Bioneers
      • US Social Justice Forum
      • SXSW
      • TED 
    • Question:
      • Are there other people at P2PU interested in doing this (or something similar)?
      • How soon do we make the big push on this?
        • Do I pick a few smaller conferences to try this out? Or go big?
      • Potential to use this model also just to get the word out (we should consider doing this for other areas - and get P2PU staff and volunteers involved)
      • Pitched this idea to 40-50 people and everyone responds positively
      • What can make this fail?
        • Sponsorship issues - how much can we promote corporate sponsors (if we don't like them)?
        • Running a really bad course with lots of attention - social innovation space is small enough that word will get around
        • Not having someone really drive this (feel ownership, wake up in the morning worrying about making it awesome)
